<p><p>Sputtering targets for photovoltaic cells are crucial materials used in the thin-film deposition process. The two main types include metal targets and alloy targets. Metal targets consist of pure metals like silver, copper, or aluminum, which provide excellent conductivity and efficiency. Alloy targets, on the other hand, combine different metals to enhance specific properties, such as stability or improved adhesion. Both types are essential in creating high-performance photovoltaic cells that efficiently convert solar energy into electricity.</p></p>
