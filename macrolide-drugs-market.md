<p><p>Macrolide drugs are a class of antibiotics characterized by a large lactone ring structure. They are categorized into three main types based on the size of their ring: 14-membered ring agents (e.g., erythromycin), 15-membered ring agents (e.g., azithromycin), and 16-membered ring agents (e.g., telithromycin). Ketolides, a subclass, are derived from macrolides with modifications for improved efficacy against resistant bacteria. These distinctions influence their antibacterial spectrum, pharmacokinetics, and clinical applications in treating various infections.</p></p>
