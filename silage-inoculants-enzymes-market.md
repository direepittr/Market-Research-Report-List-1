<p><p>Silage inoculants and enzymes are additives used to enhance the fermentation process of silage. The market is classified into heterofermentative and homofermentative types. Heterofermentative inoculants produce a range of byproducts, including lactic acid, acetic acid, and ethanol, aiding in the preservation of silage and reducing spoilage. In contrast, homofermentative inoculants primarily produce lactic acid, promoting a more efficient fermentation process. Both types aim to improve the nutritional value, digestibility, and shelf-life of silage for livestock feed.</p></p>
