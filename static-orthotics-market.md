<p><ul><li>Upper Limb Orthotics</li><li>Lower Limb Orthotics</li><li>Spinal Orthotics</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The static orthotics market is categorized into three main types: upper limb orthotics, lower limb orthotics, and spinal orthotics. Upper limb orthotics are designed to support and immobilize arms and hands, aiding in recovery from injuries. Lower limb orthotics focus on the legs and feet, helping with alignment and function, often used in conditions like arthritis or post-surgery. Spinal orthotics are geared towards stabilizing the spine, providing support for conditions such as scoliosis or post-operative recovery, enhancing overall mobility and comfort.</p></p>
