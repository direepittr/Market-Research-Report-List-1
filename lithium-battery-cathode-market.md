<p><p>The lithium battery cathode market encompasses various types, each with unique properties and applications. LCO (Lithium Cobalt Oxide) is known for high energy density, ideal for smartphones. LMO (Lithium Manganese Oxide) offers thermal stability and safety, often used in power tools. LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate) prioritizes safety and lifespan, suitable for electric vehicles. NCM (Nickel Cobalt Manganese) combines energy density and stability, popular in EVs. NCA (Nickel Cobalt Aluminum) focuses on high capacity, commonly found in high-performance EVs.</p></p>
