This process employs electrodes to create an electric field, driving the movement of ions through selectively permeable membranes. <p><p>The Electrode Ionization market features two primary construction types: Plate and Frame, and Spiral Wound. Plate and Frame construction involves a series of flat electrodes separated by spacers, allowing for efficient ion exchange and easy maintenance. In contrast, Spiral Wound construction comprises a coiled configuration, optimizing space and enhancing surface area for effective ionization. Both designs cater to varying operational needs, with Plate and Frame offering modularity and flexibility, while Spiral Wound provides compactness and improved performance in ionization processes.</p></p>
