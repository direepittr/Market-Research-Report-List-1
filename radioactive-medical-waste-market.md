<p><p>The radioactive medical waste market is categorized into three types based on radiation levels. Low-level waste (LLW) includes items like gloves and gowns, emitting negligible radiation and requiring minimal handling. Intermediate-level waste (ILW) contains higher radiation levels, such as contaminated equipment, needing shielding during storage and transport. High-level waste (HLW) consists of spent nuclear fuel and highly radioactive materials that demand extensive containment and cooling systems due to their potential hazards, necessitating specialized disposal solutions.</p></p>
