<p><p>The binaural cochlear implant market comprises two primary types: body-worn cochlear implants and ear-worn cochlear implants. Body-worn cochlear implants are externally mounted devices that connect to a processor worn on the body, often offering enhanced sound processing capabilities. In contrast, ear-worn cochlear implants resemble conventional hearing aids, providing a more discreet option for users. Both types aim to improve auditory experiences for individuals with severe hearing loss, catering to different preferences and lifestyle needs.</p></p>
