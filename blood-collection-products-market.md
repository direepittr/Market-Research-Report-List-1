<p><ul><li>Serum Separating Tubes</li><li>EDTA Tubes</li><li>Plasma Separation Tube</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The blood collection products market includes various types of tubes essential for sample processing. Serum separating tubes are designed to facilitate serum collection by allowing blood to clot and separating the serum upon centrifugation. EDTA tubes contain a preservative that prevents blood coagulation, making them ideal for complete blood counts and other hematological tests. Plasma separation tubes, equipped with gel separators, efficiently isolate plasma for biochemical analysis. Each type plays a critical role in ensuring accurate diagnostics and effective patient care.</p></p>

<p><p>The blood collection products market encompasses two primary applications: venous blood collection and capillary blood collection. Venous blood collection involves drawing blood from larger veins, typically for diagnostic tests, research, and transfusions. It is widely used in hospitals and clinics. Capillary blood collection, on the other hand, involves obtaining blood from smaller vessels, often through finger or heel pricks, making it suitable for point-of-care testing and at-home monitoring. Both methods are critical for accurate diagnostics and patient management.</p></p>
